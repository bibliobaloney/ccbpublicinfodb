V. CLAIMANT RESPONDENT The Copyright Claims Board (Board) finds that your claim does not comply with the requirements of the CASE Act and related regulations. The claim cannot move forward unless it is amended. If you wish to proceed with this claim, you must file an amended claim by April 22, 2024. If you do not, the Board must dismiss your claim without prejudice, although you may file again in the future before the Board or in federal court. If you file an amended claim and it is found compliant, you will be notified and directed to proceed with service. There is no additional filing fee for an amended claim. 17 U.S.C. § 1506(f)(1)(B). To make your amended claim compliant, you must resolve the issues identified below. 37 C.F.R. § 224.1(c)(2). You may also choose to correct or edit any errors or other information in your claim before you file it again. You do not need to provide a legal argument in your claim – just a statement of facts and circumstances. Being specific in your description gives the other party and the Board more information about your claim. There is no character limit, so please be as detailed as possible. Actual Claim or Controversy You have alleged a claim of copyright infringement; however, in your claim you have included a supplementary document that shows correspondence between you and the respondent offering to waive liability. In the supplementary document, you state “[i]f you agree to participate [in a CCB proceeding], I can offer you immunity.” It is unclear from the correspondence you have included if the respondent replied to that message, whether through acceptance, rejection or otherwise, regarding the offer. If the respondent accepted your offer, you may not have a genuine legal dispute that the Board can resolve. Even if the respondent remained silent on your offer, the existence of the offer may still mean that you do not have a genuine legal dispute that the Board can resolve as, if the Board were to find the claim compliant and allow you to serve the respondent, the respondent will have technically accepted your offer by not opting out of the claim, and there will be nothing for the Board to decide. If there is no controversy for the Board to decide, your claim must be dismissed. You should only amend your claim if you can demonstrate that the respondent rejected your offer or your offer otherwise expired.
