V. CLAIMANTS RESPONDENT The Copyright Claims Board (Board) finds that your claim does not comply with the requirements of the CASE Act and related regulations. The claim cannot move forward unless it is amended. If you wish to proceed with this claim, you must file an amended claim by July 22, 2024. If you do not, the Board must dismiss your claim without prejudice, although you may file again in the future before the Board or in federal court. If you file an amended claim and it is found compliant, you will be notified and directed to proceed with service. There is no additional filing fee for an amended claim. 17 U.S.C. § 1506(f)(1)(B). To make your amended claim compliant, you must resolve the issues identified below. 37 C.F.R. § 224.1(c)(2). You may also choose to correct or edit any errors or other information in your claim before you file it again. You do not need to provide a legal argument in your claim – just a statement of facts and circumstances. Being specific in your description gives the other party and the Board more information about your claim. There is no character limit, so please be as detailed as possible. Proper Claimant You have filed a claim for a declaration of noninfringement, naming two claimants, Washington & Company LLC (W&C) and David Washington. Your claim does not indicate why W & C is a proper claimant. A claim for a declaration of noninfringement asks the Board to find that your actions do not amount to an infringement of the respondent’s copyrighted work. 17 U.S.C. § 1504(c)(2). If someone has accused you of infringing their copyright, a noninfringement claim requests an official declaration that you did not infringe. The claim must identify the party that accused you of infringement and must describe your allegedly infringing activity, the reasons why you believe no infringement occurred, and the reasons why you believe there is an actual controversy about the requested declaration. 37 C.F.R. §§ 222.2(c)(8)(i) & (iii)-(v). Your noninfringement claim seems to meet those requirements for David Washington, but not for W & C. You allege that the respondent accused David Washington of infringement, and the cease-and-desist emails filed with the claim show those accusations. However, it does not appear that respondent has accused W & C of infringement. In other words, W & C does not appear to be a party in a dispute with the respondent regarding its alleged infringement of the respondent’s copyright. ORDER TO AMEND NONCOMPLIANT CLAIM
