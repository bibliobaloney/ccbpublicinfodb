V. CLAIMANT RESPONDENT The Copyright Claims Board finds that your claim does not comply with the requirements of the CASE Act and related regulations. The claim cannot move forward unless it is amended. If you wish to proceed with this claim, you must file an amended claim by July 1, 2024. If you do not, the Board must dismiss your claim without prejudice, although you may file again in the future before the Board or in federal court. If you file an amended claim and it is found compliant, you will be notified and directed to proceed with service. There is no additional filing fee for an amended claim. 17 U.S.C. § 1506(f)(1)(B). To make your amended claim compliant, you must resolve the issues identified below. 37 C.F.R. § 224.1(c)(2). You may also choose to correct or edit any errors or other information in your claim before you file it again. You do not need to provide a legal argument in your claim – just a statement of facts and circumstances. Being specific in your description gives the other party and the Board more information about your claim. There is no character limit, so please be as detailed as possible. Copyright Registration You have raised a claim of copyright infringement before the Board. The claim must include the copyright registration number and effective date of registration for the allegedly infringed work or, if it has not yet been registered, the service request number for a pending application to register the copyright. 37 C.F.R. §§ 221.1(b) & 222.2(c)(7)(ii)(C)-(D). Your claim correctly listed the service request number for an application for registration for a literary work. That registration has since issued. If you submit an amended claim, use the drop-down menu in the “Works infringed” section to state that the copyright has been registered, and include the registration number TX0009380036 and the March 9, 2024 effective date of registration. Claimant Name and Address A claim before the Board must include the claimant’s name and mailing address. 37 C.F.R. § 222.2(c)(2). If the claimant is represented by an attorney, the claim must also include the attorney’s mailing address. Id. § 222.2(c)(3). The claim provides Sen LinYu as claimant’s name. The copyright registration certificate for the allegedly infringed work indicates that Sen LinYu is a pseudonym, not claimant’s legal name. The claim also includes an address for claimant’s attorney, but not claimant’s address. The Board raised these issues in an Order to Show ORDER TO AMEND NONCOMPLIANT CLAIM
