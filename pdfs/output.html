V. CLAIMANT RESPONDENT The Copyright Claims Board finds that your claim, as amended, still does not comply with the requirements of the CASE Act and related regulations. The claim cannot move forward unless it is properly amended. If you wish to proceed with this claim, you must file a second amended claim by April 5, 2024. If you do not, the Board must dismiss your claim without prejudice, although you may file again in the future before the Board or in federal court. There is no additional filing fee for an amended claim. If you file a second amended claim and it is found compliant, you will be notified and directed to proceed with service. However, if your second amended claim also is found not to comply, the Board will dismiss the proceeding without prejudice. 17 U.S.C. § 1506(f)(1)(B); 37 C.F.R. § 224.1(d). To make your second amended claim compliant, you must resolve the issues identified below. 37 C.F.R. § 224.1(c)(2). You may also choose to correct or edit any errors or other information in your claim before you file it again. You do not need to provide a legal argument in your claim – just a statement of facts and circumstances. Being specific in your description gives the other party and the Board more information about your claim. There is no character limit, so please be as detailed as possible. Allegedly Infringed Work If you submit a second amended claim, it must clarify certain discrepancies between the work identified in your copyright registration and the allegedly infringed images you identify in the amended claim. You identify the allegedly infringed work as registered with Registration No. VA 2-379-026. As stated in the February 5, 2024 Order to Amend Noncompliant Claim (“February 5 Order”): The registration covers your authorship in photographic works, specifically, in the photographs deposited with the Copyright Office to support your application for registration. A Board review of Copyright Office records found that your deposit was a 471-page PDF, with the file name “owntheavenue_catalogue_2023-2024_v5.pdf,” which appears to include more than 100 screenshots of the website OwntheAvenue.com. The screenshots depict thousands of items of merchandise, primarily decals, stickers, and license plate frames. In the “Works infringed” section of the amended claim, the title of the allegedly infringed work is “Pages 1-10 of exact photos ripped from OwnTheAvenue.com.” In the “Describe the work” section, you state: “Ripped entire image of license plate frames and put it into their catalogue - Exact image from our website.” In the “Describe the SECOND ORDER TO AMEND NONCOMPLIANT CLAIM

Supplemental Documents It is not clear how several of the supplemental documents included with your amended claim support or relate to the infringing activity alleged in your amended claim. If you submit a second amended claim, please include only documents that directly pertain or relate to the claim, and provide an explanation of their relevance if it is not clear. Supplemental documents labeled Bigfoot Research Team Mountains Frame Display.jpg, CreationDateAndTimeDataEvidence.jpg, and Copyright Claims Board Infringement Sample Page.jpg show images of a “Bigfoot Research Team” license plate frame. When you first filed Claim No. 24-CCB-0003, your allegations related to an image of that license plate frame, but it is not one of the allegedly infringed images on pages 1-10 of the “Items Ripped” document at issue in the amended claim. Removing unrelated images will make it easier for the Board and the respondent to understand the nature of the claim. In addition, the CreationDateAndTimeDataEvidence.jpg file indicates that the image was created or modified on October 6, 2022, but the Certificate of Registration for VA 2-379-026 states that the registered work was completed in 2010 and first published January 1, 2011. If you do not remove that document when you amend the claim, then you must explain that apparent discrepancy, as the February 5 Order explained.
