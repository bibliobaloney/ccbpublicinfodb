V. CLAIMANTS RESPONDENT The Copyright Claims Board finds that your claim does not comply with the requirements of the CASE Act and related regulations. The claim cannot move forward unless it is amended. If you wish to proceed with this claim, you must file an amended claim by August 7, 2024. If you do not, the Board must dismiss your claim without prejudice, although you may file again in the future before the Board or in federal court. If you file an amended claim and it is found compliant, you will be notified and directed to proceed with service. There is no additional filing fee for an amended claim. 17 U.S.C. § 1506(f)(1)(B). To make your amended claim compliant, you must resolve the issues identified below. 37 C.F.R. § 224.1(c)(2). You may also choose to correct or edit any errors or other information in your claim before you file it again. You do not need to provide a legal argument in your claim – just a statement of facts and circumstances. Being specific in your description gives the other party and the Board more information about your claim. There is no character limit, so please be as detailed as possible. Respondent – Clarity In the Respondent section of the claim form, you listed the respondent’s name as Activechuckss/ Lamonte Sims. In response to the question, “Is the respondent an organization or an individual?” you answered, “Organization.” However, your allegations seem to describe infringement by an individual, not an organization. If you file an amended claim, you must clarify the Respondent section. For example, if the respondent is a person named Lamonte Sims, select “Individual” in response to that question; and if Activechuckss is that person’s alias or pseudonym, enter it in the “Alias” field. However, if Activechuckss is a separate entity from Lamonte Sims, and you are bringing a claim of infringement against both of them, then you should create separate entries for each of them in the Respondent section by clicking the “Add respondent” button again, and provide the identifying information required for each respondent. Infringing Activity – Clarity Your claim does not clearly state how the respondent used your exclusive rights in the allegedly infringed work without permission. Copyright law grants exclusive rights to copyright owners. You state that the respondent “recorded and reproduce my video as an original body of work, on his Youtube channel,” and “record my work and incorporated my video to his own video and reproduce it on his Youtube channel as an original piece of work.” Those allegations do not include enough clarity about how you allege that your work was used. When you say that the respondent “incorporated my video to his own video,” it is not clear if you mean that the respondent’s video was nothing but a copy of your video, or if it was a derivative work that also included original work or other material by the respondent, or if the respondent reproduced your entire video or just incorporated a portion of it (and if it was only a portion, which portion). If you file an amended claim, provide sufficient details for the Board and the respondent to understand what the allegedly infringing acts were and how your work and the allegedly infringed work are substantially similar to each other. Please include a clear, detailed statement regarding which portions of your work were taken by the respondent in the allegedly infringing work, including how the allegedly infringing work is similar to yours, or documents that are sufficient to show the similarities. If you include copies of the works at issue as supplemental documents, they must be clearly labeled and identified so they can be properly compared to each other. Relief Sought In the “Description of harm suffered and relief sought” section, you allege that the infringement caused you “[p]sychological and emotional damage” and “stress and anxiety.” The Board cannot award damages based on emotional or psychological harm. If you file an amended claim, there is no reason to include such allegations. Information about the types of relief available for an infringement claim is available at pages 6-7 and 16-17 of the Starting an Infringement Claim chapter of the CCB Handbook.
